Founded as a non-profit research lab, OpenAI pivoted to a capped-profit model and has since become the most valuable private AI company in the world. Their ChatGPT brought AI to the mainstream — reaching 100 million users in just 2 months — and their GPT series has consistently set the pace for the industry.

With $19-20 billion in annual recurring revenue, OpenAI is the clear revenue leader. Their model lineup spans from GPT-5 (their most capable flagship) to o3/o4-mini (reasoning specialists), plus specialized models like GPT-4.1 for coding. OpenAI's strategy is aggressive: scale the models, scale the user base, scale the infrastructure.

Key strategic move: The $500 billion Stargate infrastructure partnership with SoftBank and Oracle — the largest AI infrastructure project ever announced. This signals OpenAI's belief that the next breakthroughs will come from massive compute, and they're securing it before competitors can.

Founded by former OpenAI researchers Dario and Daniela Amodei, Anthropic was born from a conviction that AI safety needed to be a first-class priority, not an afterthought. Their Claude models compete at the frontier while emphasizing safety through Constitutional AI — a technique where the model is trained against a set of principles rather than just human feedback.

Anthropic's growth has been staggering: 10x annual revenue growth for 3 consecutive years, reaching $14 billion ARR. Their Claude models are known for strong coding, long-context understanding (200K tokens), and agentic capabilities. Claude Code alone generates over $2.5 billion in revenue.

Key strategic move: Betting heavily on AI agents and safety research. While others race to build the biggest model, Anthropic is building models that can autonomously complete multi-step tasks (agents) while maintaining the strongest safety guardrails in the industry. Their thesis: the company that solves safe autonomy wins the long game.

Created from the 2023 merger of Google Brain and DeepMind, Google DeepMind has the deepest research bench in AI. They literally invented the Transformer (the 2017 paper came from Google researchers). Their legacy includes AlphaGo, AlphaFold (protein folding), and now Gemini — their flagship model family.

The Gemini 3 family (now up to Gemini 3.1 Pro) leads several benchmarks, with Gemini 3 Deep Think achieving gold-medal level math performance and solving 4 previously open mathematical questions autonomously. Their massive advantage: integration across Google's ecosystem — Search, Cloud, Android, Workspace — giving them distribution that startups can only dream of.

Key strategic move: Integration, not isolation. While OpenAI and Anthropic sell models via API, Google embeds AI directly into products used by billions: AI Overviews in Search, Gemini in Gmail, AI agents in Google Cloud. Their bet is that AI embedded everywhere beats AI as a standalone product.

Meta's Llama models are the most successful open-source AI models in history. By releasing their models freely, Meta has created an enormous ecosystem of developers, researchers, and companies building on top of Llama. Their latest, Llama 4, uses Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture, supports 200 languages, and was trained on 30+ trillion tokens.

Meta's open-source strategy isn't altruism — it's competitive disruption. By giving away frontier-quality models for free, they commoditize the model layer, making it harder for competitors to charge premium prices for API access. Meanwhile, Meta benefits from community improvements and integrates AI into Instagram, WhatsApp, and Facebook.

Key strategic move: Commoditize the complement. If AI models are free, the value shifts to data (Meta has billions of users), distribution (3.9B monthly active people), and applications. Open-source Llama weakens OpenAI's and Google's pricing power while strengthening Meta's platform moat.

Europe's champion. Founded in 2023 by ex-Meta and ex-DeepMind researchers. Known for the fastest inference among frontier models. CEO Arthur Mensch's philosophy: "Too many GPUs makes you lazy." The wild card. Elon Musk's AI company, launched in 2023. Their Grok 4.1 holds the #1 position on LMArena with a 65% reduction in hallucinations. Their V3.2 model integrates thinking into tool-use and achieves gold-level results in IMO and ICPC. V4 (multimodal: text, image, video) is imminent. Their success challenged the "just throw more compute at it" thesis.

Not the flashiest, but quietly building a large enterprise business.
The Big Debate

Open Source vs. Closed Source

The defining strategic question of the AI industry

Open Source

Llama · Mistral · DeepSeek · Qwen
  • Free to use and modify — no API costs, no vendor lock-in
  • Full control over data — nothing leaves your servers
  • Community innovation — thousands of developers improve the models
  • Fine-tuning freedom — customize for your specific use case
  • Requires infrastructure — you need GPUs to run them
  • Safety guardrails vary — less consistent than managed APIs

Closed Source

GPT · Claude · Gemini
  • Cutting-edge performance — usually 6-12 months ahead
  • Easy API access — no infrastructure to manage
  • Safety guardrails built in — consistent, well-tested
  • Continuous improvement — models get better without you doing anything
  • Vendor lock-in risk — dependent on provider's pricing and policies
  • Data privacy concerns — queries may be processed on provider's servers
The trend: Open-source models are rapidly closing the gap. Llama 4 and DeepSeek V3.2 now match or beat GPT-4 on many benchmarks. The performance difference between the top 10 models shrank from 11.9% to just 5.4% in 2024. As open models approach frontier quality, the value proposition of closed models shifts from "better performance" to "easier integration, better safety, and managed infrastructure." The future likely isn't one or the other — it's both, for different use cases.
